Cosmic Cities

Galaxies are massive collections of gas, dust, and billions of stars.

Andromeda Galaxy (M31)

Spiral Galaxy • 2.537 million light-years away

Our Milky Way's largest neighbor. It is on a collision course with us and will merge with the Milky Way in about 4.5 billion years.

Sombrero Galaxy (M104)

Unbarred Spiral • 29 million light-years away

Famous for its bright nucleus and a large central bulge that looks like a hat. It has a supermassive black hole at its center.

Whirlpool Galaxy (M51)

Spiral Galaxy • 23 million light-years away

A classic spiral galaxy that is actually interacting with a smaller dwarf galaxy. It’s a favorite target for amateur astronomers.

Triangulum Galaxy (M33)

Spiral Galaxy • 2.73 million light-years away

The third-largest member of the Local Group (after Andromeda and the Milky Way).

Eyes in the Sky

Highlighting major missions from ISRO (India) and global agencies.

Aryabhata

ISRO • Launched 1975

India's very first satellite, named after the famous astronomer. It marked the beginning of India's journey into space exploration.

Chandrayaan-3

ISRO • Lunar Mission • 2023

A historic success! It made India the first nation to land near the Lunar South Pole, searching for water ice and studying the moon's surface.

Mangalyaan (MOM)

ISRO • Mars Orbiter • 2013

India became the first nation to reach Mars orbit on its first attempt, and at a budget lower than the cost of the movie 'Gravity'.

Aditya-L1

ISRO • Solar Mission • 2023

India's first space-based observatory to study the Sun. It orbits at Lagrange Point 1 to observe solar coronas.

James Webb Telescope

NASA/ESA/CSA • 2021

The most powerful space telescope ever built. It uses infrared light to see the very first galaxies formed after the Big Bang.

Indian Space Heroes

The visionaries who put India on the cosmic map.

Vikram Sarabhai

Father of the Indian Space Program

A physicist and astronomer who initiated space research in India. He helped set up ISRO and believed space tech should improve common lives.

Satish Dhawan

Chairman of ISRO (1972-1984)

He led the development of operational launch vehicles like SLV-3. Known for taking responsibility for failures and giving credit for success.

A. P. J. Abdul Kalam

Rocket Scientist & Former President

Known as the "Missile Man of India," he played a pivotal role in the development of India's ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology.

Kalpana Chawla

First Woman of Indian Origin in Space

An American astronaut and engineer who flew on Space Shuttle Columbia. She remains an inspiration for millions of girls worldwide.

Ritu Karidhal

Rocket Woman of India

A senior scientist at ISRO, she was the Deputy Operations Director for Mangalyaan, playing a key role in navigating the spacecraft to Mars.
